Independence in Substance

A director quota is met. A board is strengthened.

It is one thing to satisfy a requirement for independent representation. It is another to seat directors whose independence is real — in judgment, in standing, and in their willingness to ask the question others will not.

We approach independent director search as a governance question before a search question. We begin with what the board genuinely lacks — a perspective, an expertise, a counterweight — and identify the individuals who supply it with credibility, not merely with eligibility.

The distinction matters most precisely when it is least convenient — in the decisions a board would rather not examine too closely.

The Qatari Context

Fluent in Qatar's governance expectations.

Recent governance reform has raised the bar for independent representation on the boards of companies listed in Qatar, alongside stronger expectations on independence criteria and board capability. For many boards, this is not a formality to satisfy but an opportunity to strengthen.

We read these expectations as a leadership question — the right directors, brought in for the right reasons — and we shape each mandate around the board's specific position within them.

We offer this as strategic context for board decisions, not as legal or regulatory advice.
